# Holiday Greeting Video

Turns the exclusive atmosphere of a holiday or occasion into an emotional short video with greeting text. Core: visuals fully match the holiday vibe, greeting text naturally integrated into the frame, emotion warm but not cheesy.

## Key Principles

- **Atmosphere first**: the first shot must make viewers immediately feel what holiday/occasion this is
- **Greeting text must appear on screen**: can't rely on narration alone — subtitles/text effects are required
- **Make holiday elements specific**: not generic "warm scenes" — use the holiday's iconic visual symbols
- **Emotional closing**: the last shot should feel spacious, giving viewers room to absorb the emotion
- **No tail frames; audio before video**

## Workflow

### 1. Confirm Holiday Information, Determine Visual Direction

**Required info**: holiday/occasion name + recipient (optional) + personalized content (optional).

**Common holiday visual elements reference**:

| Holiday / Occasion | Iconic Elements | Color Tone |
|---|---|---|
| Chinese New Year | Red lanterns, fireworks, plum blossoms, couplets, gold | Red + gold |
| Christmas | Snow, Christmas tree, gifts, reindeer, fireplace | Red + green + white |
| Birthday | Birthday cake & candles, balloons, ribbons, gift boxes | Colorful / warm tones |
| Wedding / Anniversary | Bouquets, rings, rose petals, champagne, candlelight | White + pink / gold |
| Graduation | Graduation cap, diploma, campus, flying caps | Blue + gold / school colors |
| Mother's/Father's Day | Bouquets, family scenes, warm hugs | Warm tones / floral colors |
| Valentine's Day | Roses, hearts, candlelit dinner, gifts | Red + pink |
| Lantern Festival | Flower lanterns, tangyuan, fireworks | Red + orange + gold |

**For holidays without standard references**: infer core symbols and emotional color tone from the holiday name, and determine visual direction directly.

### 2. Plan Shot Structure

**Standard structure** (5–7 shots, 20–40 seconds):

| Shot | Content | Duration |
|---|---|---|
| 1 | Holiday atmosphere opening: iconic holiday scene, establish the festive feel | 3–5s |
| 2–3 | Holiday element showcase: close-ups or scenes of the holiday's core visual symbols | 3–4s each |
| 4 | Greeting text shot: large subtitle as the main visual, with holiday background | 3–5s |
| 5–6 | Emotional scene: warm person/scene imagery, convey emotion | 3–4s each |
| 7 | Closing shot: spacious feel, with final greeting or holiday name | 3–4s |

### 5. Generate Audio

**BGM**: matches holiday emotion, primarily warm, not overly noisy.

| Holiday | BGM Example |
|---|---|
| Chinese New Year / traditional | Cheerful folk music, festive feel, BPM 100–120 |
| Christmas / Western holidays | Bells + piano, warm and cheerful |
| Birthday | Light and joyful, fresh feel |
| Wedding / anniversary | Romantic piano or strings, gentle |
| Graduation | Inspirational feel, slightly powerful, emotionally rich |

**Narration** (optional): brief greeting, warm tone. If subtitles fully convey the message, narration can be omitted.
