# Mystery Story Short

Turns a mystery story or plot into an atmospheric narrative short. Core: use visual atmosphere, narrative pacing, and information control to build suspense — leaving viewers with a strong "need to know the answer" impulse at the end.

## Key Principles

- **Never give away too much information**: each shot only reveals a small part of the story, maintaining viewer curiosity
- **Atmosphere is the primary productivity**: lighting, color tone, and ambient sound carry 50% of the mystery feel
- **Rhythm needs to breathe**: don't rush the whole video forward — slow pace + sudden fast cuts = maximum mystery
- **Twist must be set up first**: viewers can look back and see the hints were there all along, but didn't notice at the time
- **Ending must leave a question**: close with a question sentence, unexplained image, or sudden interruption
- **No tail frames; audio before video**

## Workflow

### 1. Extract Story Core, Plan Shot List

**Required info**: mystery theme/story outline + core twist (what is the twist).

**Shot structure** (8–12 shots, 45–90 seconds):

| Segment | Shot Count | Rhythm | Purpose |
|---|---|---|---|
| Atmosphere build | 2–3 | Slow (4–6s) | Establish oppressive/eerie environmental atmosphere, explain nothing |
| Normal disrupted | 1–2 | Medium (3–4s) | Show the first signal that "something's wrong" |
| Clue laying | 3–4 | Medium to fast (2–4s) | Each shot has one clue, but doesn't explain its meaning |
| Climax twist | 2–3 | Fast cuts (1–2s) then slow-mo | Truth revealed in an instant, emotional peak |
| Suspense ending | 1–2 | Extremely slow or still (3–5s) | Leave the question, image fades out or hard cut to black |

**Clue design principles**:
- Clues must genuinely exist in the visuals (props, environmental details, character behavior) — don't explain through narration
- Each clue shot looks ordinary; its meaning only becomes clear in conjunction with the twist
- Maximum 3 clues — too many feels verbose

### 2. Send Shot List

```
Art style: cinematic, low saturation, cool tones (blue-grey / dark green / dark purple), high-contrast lighting

[Global constraints]
- Color tone: low saturation throughout, gloomy feel, not warm colors
- Composition: unsettling feel — diagonal lines, oppressive space, blurry unidentifiable objects in background
- No bright cheerful scenes
- Subtitles (if any): white or grey small text at the bottom of screen

Shot 1 (slow, Xs): [oppressive environment description, no character or character with back turned], narration/subtitle: "[opening atmosphere line]"
Shot 2 (slow, Xs): [abnormal signal appears], narration: none, subtitle: none (let the visual speak)
Shot 3 (medium, Xs): [first clue], narration: "[describe the surface appearance without explaining clue meaning]"
...
Shot N (slow, Xs): [suspense ending visual, question unresolved], subtitle: "[question / unfinished statement]"
```

**Narration style**:
- First person narration, detached feel, like remembering the past
- Do not directly explain the clue meaning
- Ending narration can cut off suddenly

### 3. Filter Check

- Is low-saturation cool tone maintained throughout
- Do clue shots avoid explaining their meaning prematurely
- Does the ending leave an unanswered question

### 5. Generate Audio

**BGM formula**: `low-frequency strings + irregular rhythm + sudden silence`

| Segment | BGM Treatment |
|---|---|
| Atmosphere build | Extremely low volume strings/piano, barely audible |
| Clue laying | Gradually intensifying, irregular rhythm creates unease |
| Climax twist | Sudden impact sound effect or music collapse feel |
| Suspense ending | Sudden silence, or one sustained single note fading out |

**Narration voice**: suppressed, calm, slightly mechanical — deliberately emotionally restrained.
